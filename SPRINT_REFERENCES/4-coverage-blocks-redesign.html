What changed vs. today's steps

  1. Assets step tells you WHY it exists — "blocks in the next step attach to what you pick here." Today that linkage is never explained.
  2. One question at a time — attachment mode first (with "client lists them after signing" as a first-class card, not a buried toggle), then coverage in plain language, then assets only if relevant.
  3. Coverage types lead — they're the mandatory thing, so they come before the equipment grid, each with a one-line meaning.
  4. Registry collapses once picked — "4 assets selected · edit" instead of a permanent grid.
  5. Blocks: recommendations open the step — VaNi suggests from nomenclature + coverage; one click adds the set. The catalog below is a checklist; config unfolds only for selected blocks.
  6. Cycle mismatch warns at selection — inline on the block, not a toast at Continue.
  7. Price edits become recorded discounts — "list → your price (−%)" display; groundwork for the discounts feature.
  8. Payoff screen — blocks grouped under coverage types with assets counted, so the asset↔block linkage the data model already has finally becomes visible.
