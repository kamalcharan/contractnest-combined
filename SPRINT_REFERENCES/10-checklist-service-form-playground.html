What this playground settles

  1. Today, Smart Forms is a blank-slate builder disconnected from equipment/facility context. This proposes starting from an equipment pick instead.
  2. One Knowledge Tree selection generates two purpose-built artifacts — a Checklist (the technician's fast field companion) and a Service Form (the fuller record, which embeds the checklist rather than repeating it).
  3. Generation is a one-time snapshot, not a live sync — matches the existing "clone & edit" intent. Editing the checklist/form afterward doesn't reach back into the Knowledge Tree.
  4. "Facility" = the asset catalog category, aliased for the field team. Equipment and Facility are the two categories with any checkpoint coverage today (Team & Staff, Service, Consumable have none).
  5. Stage 4 & 5 show where this plugs into what already exists — the Evidence Policy wizard step and the Start Service drawer are real, live components today; this shows the gaps in them, not a rebuild.

Still open

  1. Is a Checklist+Form pair generated for every equipment pick, or does the equipment type decide which of the two actually makes sense?
  2. Should the field checklist look this distinct from a generic form, or is that visual investment for later?
