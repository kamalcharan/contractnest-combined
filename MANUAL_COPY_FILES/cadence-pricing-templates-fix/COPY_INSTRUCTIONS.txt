ROOT CAUSE (verified against your live DB)
  Your contract was instantiated from the signed-off template via the
  composer fast path in contractnest-api. That path rebuilt each
  template block into a synthetic candidate with a hard-coded field
  list — silently DROPPING config (cadence rate card, seller final
  payment, billingOnly) and recomputing total as rate × qty:
    - t_contract_blocks.total_price = 4,200 (rate, not the 16,800 term)
    - persisted config had only {notes, billingOnly, showDescription}
    - events fell to the generic quarterly split: ceil(365/90) = 5
      payments of 840 (= 4,200 ÷ 5)
  The wizard-side math from the previous batch was correct — the
  server-side instantiation path bypassed it. My earlier decision to
  defer block-config persistence to step 2c was wrong: templates
  round-trip through persistence immediately. This batch brings that
  forward.

WHAT'S FIXED
 A. contractnest-api / contractComposerService
    - Template candidates now carry the FULL saved block config
      (cadence rate card, seller final payment, billingOnly, notes).
    - Cadence blocks keep their saved payment cadence and their
      total is the TERM total (4 × 4,200 = 16,800), never rate × qty.
    - Contract subtotal/tax/grand total compute off term totals.
 B. contractnest-api / contractEventsDerivationService (+ parity test)
    - Billing-only blocks no longer generate service events (parity
      with the UI — this was missing server-side even before cadence).
    - Cadence billing events: N payments at the rate + the seller-set
      final payment as its own labeled event; halfyearly/annual map
      to 182/365 days.
    - Parity test extended with 3 cadence scenarios — 9/9 scenarios
      byte-identical (including "billing-only cadence block → exactly
      4 quarterly payments, no service events").
 C. contractnest-ui / review page + wizard Review step
    - Billing-only blocks now group under BILLING, never "Service".
    - Cadence blocks show "4 payments × ₹4,200 · Quarterly" instead
      of "Qty: 1", and the per-unit footer reads "₹4,200/qtr".
    - Review step Subtotal now uses term totals (was showing the
      bare rate, e.g. ₹4,200 while Grand Total said ₹16,800).
    - Mixed Billing Schedule row: term totals no longer mislabeled
      with a "/qtr" suffix (now says "term total").
