WHAT THIS DELIVERS
When a CADENCE-PRICED block (from step 1's catalog rate card) is
added to a contract or template:

 1. The block card's "Billing Cycle" picker is replaced by PAYMENT
    CADENCE chips — one per enabled cadence that FITS the contract's
    duration, each showing its rate ("Quarterly · ₹4,500 per
    quarter"), with the tenant default badged and pre-selected.
    Labeled "your proposal to the buyer".
 2. A payment schedule summary computes live:
    "4 payments × ₹4,500 = ₹18,000 over 12 months".
 3. Leftover months (e.g. 8-month contract, quarterly cadence) show
    the SELLER-SET final payment field — pro-rata offered only as a
    clickable suggestion, never applied silently (locked decision).
 4. Selling-price overrides are per-cadence and survive switching
    cadences (negotiate ₹4,200 on Quarterly, flip to Monthly and
    back — the ₹4,200 is remembered). The recorded-discount chip
    compares against the CHOSEN cadence's list rate.
 5. Visits stay decoupled: quantity/service-cycle keep driving
    service visits; payment count derives from (cadence, term) and is
    never stored. Changing contract duration re-derives the money —
    and if the chosen cadence no longer fits, the block auto-switches
    to the default/first fitting cadence WITH a toast (never a silent
    price change).
 6. Billing View totals, line items ("2 + final pay · Quarterly"),
    contract-level discount, EMI and the internal allocation all
    compute off the cadence term total.
 7. Events Preview generates billing events from the proposal:
    full payments at the rate (+tax), plus the seller-set final
    payment as its own labeled event. New cycles halfyearly/annual
    map to 182/365 days.

INTERIM BEHAVIOR (until steps 2b/2c)
  - The buyer does NOT yet see cadence options at review — the
    proposal is what gets sent. Buyer choice lands in 2b/2c.
  - Events preview reflects the seller's proposal (per your decision:
    it may change if the buyer picks differently at acceptance —
    that regeneration is step 2c).

NOT SUPPORTED IN V1 (flagged during design)
  - Group Session blocks ignore cadence pricing (their quantity means
    roster occurrences; payments-vs-occurrences needs its own pass)
  - If NO enabled cadence fits the term (e.g. 2-week contract, only
    quarterly priced), the block falls back to single-price at the
    anchor total
