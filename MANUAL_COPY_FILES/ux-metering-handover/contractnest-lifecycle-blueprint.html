One system · authored to serviced to settled

A contract isn't created. It's serviced.

We've made contract authoring excellent. But a service contract earns its keep in the months after signing — every visit tracked, every job proven by the right form, every completed job turning into an invoice. Today that loop has never been walked end-to-end in the live data. This blueprint stitches authoring, activation, servicing and settlement into one lifecycle — and closes the loop.

The lifecycle

Four stages, one spine

Each stage hands the next a clean baton. The strength today is lopsided — authoring and tracking are rich; the doing and the settling are stubs.

Stage 2 · Activate Works — needs a face

Acceptance turns the plan into a schedule

On acceptance the backend expands the contract's blocks + cadence into real dated rows — t_contract_events (service & billing) — each with an SLA clock. This engine works. What's missing is a moment that makes it felt: "Your contract is live — here are the 13 visits and 4 invoices it will generate."

What fires automatically

  • Service events on the weekday-anchored cadence (the new Group-Session / cadence engine)
  • Billing events on the billing cycle
  • Appointment requests auto-raised ~6 days ahead of each visit
  • SLA clock per event

The one addition

An activation summary — the same generated schedule the wizard's Events step previews, surfaced as the contract's opening state so the seller sees the commitment they just made, and the first visit is one tap away.

Stage 4 · Settle Bridge missing

Proven work becomes money — in that order

Today "Generate Invoice" is a dead button and billing runs independent of delivery. The blueprint reverses it: a completed, evidenced job is what produces the invoice, so AR always reflects real delivery — the "pay for what's done" promise, enforced.

Bridge

Billing event → invoice

Wire the stubbed generate action to the existing invoice RPC. A due billing event (or a completed service milestone) drafts the invoice.

Gate

Evidence-before-invoice

Optionally require the job's mandatory form submitted before the invoice can send — the differentiator made literal.

Ledger

AR that's true

Finance's "who owes what" already reads real invoices; once the bridge exists it reflects delivery, not intent. Feeds the metering ledger too.

The connective tissue · Smart Forms

The form is the definition of done

Each domain, tenant, equipment and facility has its own service form — the MRI calibration sheet, the HVAC PM checklist. Without the form, the service doesn't end. The schema already supports this exactly (forms bind to KT equipment via resource_template_id; mappings carry is_mandatory). It's just never been populated or enforced.
