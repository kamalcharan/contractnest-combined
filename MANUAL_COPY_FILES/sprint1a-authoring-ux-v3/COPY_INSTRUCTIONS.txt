WHY THIS BATCH EXISTS
After testing v2 live, you flagged three UX issues in the "Pick from
their registered equipment" flow. This batch addresses exactly those
three, and nothing else — the types stage, the 3 attachment cards, and
all Sprint 1a business logic are unchanged from v2.

WHAT CHANGES ON SCREEN (your 3 points, in order)
 1. Registry picker moves from BELOW the 3 attachment cards to a
    column to the RIGHT of them — sitting between the cards and the
    "Coverage" sidebar, as you asked. This only happens once you pick
    "Pick from their registered <type>" AND the picker is expanded;
    otherwise the page stays single-column exactly like before (the
    "later" and "register new" cards don't need the extra width).
 2. As soon as you attach something from the picker, it now appears
    immediately in the right sidebar under a new "Attached (N)" list
    — each item shown by name with a checkmark — so you get live
    confirmation without scrolling the picker itself.
 3. The picker now groups by equipment type and, for each type, shows
    "<total in registry> of <units you declared> in registry" as a
    badge. If the registry has fewer units than you declared (your
    "3 requested, 1 exists" example), it also shows an inline note:
    "2 more units will need to be added later (as placeholders)" right
    under that type's list — so it's clear up front which units can be
    attached now vs. which will remain placeholders no matter what you
    pick.

WHAT DOES NOT CHANGE
 - Types stage (flat cards, unit steppers) — untouched.
 - The 3 attachment-mode cards themselves — untouched, still in the
   same order/priority.
 - Placeholder reconciliation logic, unit-count math, "Register new"
   flow — untouched.
 - Collapsed one-line "N attached from the registry" summary (shown
   once you click "Done — collapse list") — untouched, still appears
   under the cards in single-column mode.
